How to Spot Phishing Attempts

Check the Sender

Verify the sender's email address carefully. Look for misspellings or suspicious domains.

Hover Over Links

Hover over links to see where they actually lead before clicking. Be suspicious of shortened URLs.

Look for Urgency

Be wary of emails that create false urgency or pressure you to act immediately.

Check for Grammar

Poor spelling and grammar are often signs of phishing attempts.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Requests for sensitive information via email
  • Unexpected attachments or downloads
  • Generic greetings like "Dear Customer"
  • Threats or urgent action required
  • Mismatched or suspicious URLs
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers
